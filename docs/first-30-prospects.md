# ProofTask First 30 Prospects

## Goal

This document defines the first 30 prospect archetypes for ProofTask outreach.

The goal is not to build a huge lead database first.

The goal is to create a focused prospect map that can drive the first 30 targeted messages and produce early discovery calls.

Primary offer:

> AI Agent QA Proof Audit: structured human proof for one AI-generated pull request, feature, or workflow before merge or release.

## Prospecting principle

Start with buyers who already have the pain.

A good prospect has at least one of these signals:

- uses AI coding tools;
- ships AI-generated pull requests;
- has a small team and limited QA capacity;
- cares about release confidence;
- needs audit evidence;
- has maintainers or reviewers overloaded by AI-generated work;
- already sells QA, testing, or agentic development services.

- 0-4: keep for later.

## First-week target

A strong first week:

- collect 30 prospects;
- send 20 messages;
- get 3-5 replies;
- book 1-2 discovery calls;
- identify 1 real AI-generated PR or feature that could be verified.

## Discovery call goal

Do not try to sell the whole vision.

Find the exact pain:

- Do they use AI coding agents?
- Do AI tools create PRs or only suggestions?
- Who verifies AI-generated changes?
- What proof is required before merge?
- Where does proof live today?
- What would make this worth paying for?

## Best first pilot candidate

The best first pilot is:

> A small team using AI coding tools weekly, shipping through GitHub, with a founder or CTO who personally worries about regressions.

Why:

- short sales cycle;
- clear pain;
- fast decision;
- concrete PR workflow;
- easier to get feedback;
- likely to pay for a focused service.

## Red flags

Avoid prospects where:

- they do not use AI coding tools;
- they only want free consulting;
- they want a full marketplace immediately;
- there is no clear buyer;
- they cannot provide one concrete PR or feature;
- they need enterprise compliance before a small pilot;
- they ask for custom platform development before validating the workflow.

## Outreach success metric

The campaign is working if at least one of these happens:

- a founder says, "We need this before merging AI PRs";
- a QA lead says, "This would help us show evidence";
- a devtool founder says, "This could fit our workflow";
- a team offers one PR or feature for a test;
- someone asks for pricing or pilot scope.

## Strategic reminder

The first 30 prospects are not about vanity metrics.

They are about market truth.

ProofTask should learn quickly:

> Who feels pain strongly enough to pay for proof?
